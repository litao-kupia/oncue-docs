# Live awareness

While you're on a call, a silent monitor watches **both sides** of the conversation and
surfaces a **quiet, fact-grounded flag** when something actually matters — so you catch the
moment without staring at the transcript.

It flags things like:

* a claim that **contradicts your notes** or what you know,
* a **commitment** being made (by either side),
* a **question to you** that went unanswered,
* a potential **risk**.

Because the flags are grounded in *your* reality — your persona facts, in-scope knowledge,
linked history, and open commitments — they're specific to you, not generic prompts.

## How it shows

A flag appears briefly in a colour-coded strip under the status bar. It auto-dismisses, or
click **✕** to clear it. It's **sparse by design** (a cheap model on a cooldown), so it stays
out of your way.

Live awareness is on whenever realtime advice is enabled. It's distinct from the other two
engines: the [advisor](advisor.md) coaches *how you're handling* the conversation, and the
reply engine drafts *what to say* — live awareness simply taps you on the shoulder when a
fact, promise, or unanswered question slips past.

## Speaker names, live

Diarized speakers ("Speaker 1/2/3") are resolved to **real names automatically**, inferred
from conversational cues like self-introductions and people addressing each other. Names now
appear **during** the call, not just after it ends — and because voice-only calls get real
names, they feed relationship memory and commitments with no manual re-scan. It's
conservative: a speaker is only renamed when their name is clearly evidenced.
