# Live awareness

A silent monitor watches both sides of the call and raises a quiet, fact-grounded flag when something matters — so you catch the moment without staring at the transcript.

It flags:

* a claim that **contradicts your notes**,
* a **commitment** made by either side,
* a **question to you** that went unanswered,
* a potential **risk**.

Flags are grounded in *your* reality — persona facts, in-scope knowledge, linked history, open commitments — so they're specific, not generic.

## How it shows

A colour-coded strip appears briefly under the status bar, then auto-dismisses (or click **✕**). It's sparse by design — a cheap model on a cooldown — so it stays out of your way. On whenever realtime advice is enabled.

Unlike the [advisor](advisor.md) (coaches *how* you're handling the call) and the reply engine (drafts *what* to say), awareness just taps you on the shoulder when a fact, promise, or unanswered question slips past.

## Speaker names, live

Diarized speakers ("Speaker 1/2/3") are resolved to real names automatically, inferred from cues like self-introductions and people addressing each other. Names appear **during** the call, so even voice-only calls feed relationship memory and commitments with no manual re-scan. Conservative: a speaker is only renamed when the name is clearly evidenced.
