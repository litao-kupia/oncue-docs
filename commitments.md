# Commitments & briefs

OnCue doesn't just remember people — it tracks what's **owed** between you and them, and
puts the right context in front of you before, during, and after a conversation. All of it
is captured automatically from your calls and channels.

## The commitments ledger

The **Commitments** tab (with an open-count badge in the nav) lists every promise made
to or from the people you talk to, grouped **You owe** / **Owed to you**, with checkboxes to
mark each one done. Commitments are extracted automatically — you don't log them.

### Due dates & urgency

When a deadline is mentioned in conversation ("by Friday"), OnCue captures it on the
commitment. The ledger then shows **overdue** and **due-soon** badges, sorts by urgency, and
counts them in the header.

### Needs attention (proactive nudges)

The Commitments tab opens with a **Needs attention** section: overdue and due-soon items you
owe, plus relationships that have gone **stale while you still owe them something**. Click a
nudge to jump straight to that person.

### One-click follow-up

Each open *you-owe* commitment has a **✉ follow-up** button that drafts a ready-to-send
message in your voice, grounded in what you know about the person. Review, edit, and copy.

## Pre-call brief

Before a conversation, get a **Pre-call brief**: who you're meeting, what you owe each other,
relationship strength, and recent context — drawn from relationship memory, your calendar,
and recent activity. Overdue and aging commitments are flagged first.

* On the **meeting-starting popup**, click **Brief me**.
* Any time, from the overlay session menu, choose **✦ Pre-call brief**.

## Daily brief

A start-of-day digest — what needs attention, who you're dealing with, your next meeting —
appears automatically once a day on launch, and is available any time from the overlay
session menu as **☀ Daily brief**.

## Post-call follow-up

When a session ends, OnCue drafts a ready-to-send **follow-up message** — recap, next steps,
and the commitments captured — in your voice. It appears in the session detail with a
**Copy follow-up** button.
