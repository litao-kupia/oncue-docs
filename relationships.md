# Relationships & memory

OnCue's core is a **living memory of the people you work with**. It builds and updates
this automatically as you talk — no manual data entry — and uses it to make every
suggestion more personal and more useful over time.

## People & dossiers

Anyone who appears in your conversations (live calls, Slack, Telegram) becomes a **person**
in OnCue. Each person accumulates a structured **dossier**:

* **Role** — what they do / their title.
* **Style** — how they communicate.
* **Temper** — their temperament and emotional tenor.
* **Characteristics** — notable traits.
* **Relationship** — your dynamic with them, read especially from your direct messages.
* **Facts** — durable, concrete details (company, projects, preferences…).

Open the **People** tab in the dashboard and click anyone to see their dossier. System
accounts (Slackbot, deactivated users, etc.) are filtered out automatically.

## The relationship graph

The **Graph** tab shows you at the center of your network, with everyone you talk to
around you — using their Slack/Telegram avatars. Connections between *other* people are
inferred from recent conversations and colour-coded by sentiment (e.g. a recent
disagreement). Hover a person for their name, click for their dossier, click a connection
to see what it's about. Zoom with the controls or **Ctrl + scroll**, and **save** or
**share** the graph as an image.

## Commitments

OnCue extracts **commitments** — promises made by you or to you — from your conversations
and tracks them per person, across channels. See them all in the **Commitments** tab
(grouped *You owe* / *Owed to you*) and check them off as you follow through. Open
commitments resurface in your pre-call briefs.

## Reckon — refresh on demand

Click **✦ Reckon** on a person (or **Reckon all**) to have OnCue rebuild their dossier from
their latest messages — including your DMs, which it weighs heavily for the relationship
read. Reckoning **merges** with what's already known: new information is preferred, but
existing details are never thrown away.

## How the memory is used

* **Replies** are grounded in the dossier of whoever you're talking to.
* **Pre-call briefs** pull together who you're meeting, what you owe each other, and recent
  context.
* **Live awareness** flags contradictions, new commitments, and unanswered questions.

It all stays **on your device**, synced to your own dashboard — never shared.
