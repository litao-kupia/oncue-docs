# Invisible on screen share

OnCue's overlay is built to stay private. It uses Windows' **display-affinity** controls, so
the overlay is **excluded from screen capture** — Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, OBS,
and the OS screen recorder all skip it.

During a shared call, the other side sees only the window or screen you chose to share —
never the OnCue overlay sitting on top of it. You read your cues on a transparent,
always-on-top layer that is yours alone.

## Hide instantly

A configurable hotkey hides the overlay immediately. Set it in **Settings → Shortcuts** in
the desktop console.

## A note on responsible use

OnCue is a tool for thinking on your feet. Use it in line with the policies of your calls,
employer, and local laws — for example, around recording or assistance during interviews
and exams.

## Privacy

Audio is transcribed to power live advice. For what's stored and how data is handled, see
the [privacy policy](https://oncue.fun/privacy).
